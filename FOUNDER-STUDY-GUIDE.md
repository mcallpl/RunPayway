## The Problem We Solve

Most people know their income *amount*. Nobody measures their income *structure*.

- A consultant earning $200K/year with 80% from one client has a **structural problem** — even though the income looks fine
- Credit scores measure borrowing history — not income resilience
- Advisors manage assets — not income architecture
- Banks track cash flow — not what happens when a source disappears

**RunPayway fills this gap.** One fixed system. One score. Reproducible every time.

## Founder Talking Points

### When someone asks "What is RunPayway?"

> "We measure how income is built — not how much someone earns. Most financial systems track what you make, what you owe, what you've saved. But nobody measures whether your income structure actually holds up when something changes — a client leaves, a deal falls through, you step away. That's what we do. One fixed system, one score, reproducible every time."

### When someone asks "How is this different from a credit score?"

> "Credit scores measure borrowing behavior — how you handle debt. We measure income structure — how your income is actually built and whether it holds under pressure. Completely different inputs, completely different purpose. We never pull credit, never access bank accounts."

### When someone asks "Do you use AI?"

> "No. The scoring model is deterministic — fixed rules, applied consistently. Same answers always produce the same score. No machine learning, no probability models, no adaptive algorithms. That's by design. When you're measuring something structural, you need consistency, not intelligence."

### When someone asks "Who is this for?"

> "Anyone whose income depends on what they do — consultants, freelancers, business owners, commission-based professionals. For individuals, it takes 2 minutes and the score is free. For advisors, it's a standardized way to assess every client's income risk. For enterprises, it's an API that brings structural income evaluation into lending, HR, and advisory platforms."

### When someone asks "How do you make money?"

> "The score is free. The full diagnostic — which shows you exactly what limits your stability, what to change, and in what order — is $69, one-time, lifetime access. For ongoing tracking, monitoring is $149/year with three assessments. For advisors and enterprises, we're rolling out per-assessment licensing."

### When someone asks about competition

> "There isn't a direct competitor because this category didn't exist before. Credit scoring measures borrowing. Advisors manage assets. Banks track cash flow. Nobody was measuring structural income — how it's built, how it holds, what breaks first. That's the gap."

### When an enterprise asks about security

> "Every assessment is stamped with the model version, timestamp, and authentication code. The methodology is version-controlled and immutable — if rules change, the version increments. We're building toward SOC 2 Type II, our practices are informed by ISO 27001, and we have a published Data Processing Agreement for GDPR. We don't access bank accounts, don't pull credit, and don't store payment card data."
